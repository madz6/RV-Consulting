# Board memo — PulseGuard commercial launch (SYNTHETIC CASE DATA)

**From:** CEO, NordMach Industrial Systems Ltd
**To:** Commercial board
**Date:** 2026-06-02

NordMach manufactures industrial air compressors and rotary equipment for UK
manufacturing plants. Over the past two years we have piloted **PulseGuard**, a
predictive-maintenance digital service that monitors vibration, temperature and
load on connected machines and schedules interventions before failures occur.

The pilot is complete. Engineering is confident the service is ready for a
commercial launch through our direct sales channel. What we have not agreed is
**how to charge for it**. Options raised internally include bundling PulseGuard
into the equipment price, a plant-level subscription, a fee per connected
machine, and a performance-linked charge tied to avoided downtime.

Customers have told us they would pay up to 20% of avoided downtime cost for a
service like this, so I believe the value is unquestionably there. Our
installed base gives us reach: roughly 420 machines across our 12 enterprise
accounts and about 880 machines across 110 mid-market plants.

Finance has flagged that our near-term cash position is tight following the
Doncaster plant investment; whatever model we choose must not require heavy
upfront subsidy. We need a recommendation the board can approve next quarter:
which pricing model, at what level, introduced how, and with what risks.

I am asking the engaged consulting team to bring us a defensible answer, not a
menu.
