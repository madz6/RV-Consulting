# Interview excerpt — Group Engineering Director, enterprise customer "Account E1" (SYNTHETIC CASE DATA)

**Interviewed:** 2026-06-16 · **Interviewer:** engagement team
**Context:** 40+ NordMach machines across 3 sites; participated in the pilot.

> "We measure downtime religiously — every stopped hour on the main line is
> about £1,800 gone. PulseGuard genuinely moved that number for us in the
> pilot."

> "Would I pay based on outcomes? In principle yes, and procurement already
> accepts availability KPIs in our service contracts. But I'd want a cap, a
> floor and a jointly owned measurement protocol. I am not signing an open-ended
> percentage of a number your model calculates."

> "What I will not do is pay a big fee per machine for the quiet ones. Half our
> fleet is backup capacity that barely runs. Charge me for the value on the
> critical machines, or charge me at site level."

> "Budget-wise this comes out of the group reliability programme, so an annual
> site-level agreement is easiest to get signed."
